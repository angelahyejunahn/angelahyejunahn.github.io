DESIGN PROCESS
1. Research
I always start with research. Depending on what I am trying to understand, I'll use one or more of the following:
  • Work with product managers to gather business requirements
  • Conduct user interviews and contextual inquiries to put myself in the users' shoes as much as possible
  • Interview sales and support team members to find out the most common pain points
  • Create user journey maps to identify gaps in existing design or process
  • Create data flows to get a sense of how the data is being transferred
  • Conduct competitive analysis to analyze how others have solved similar issues
2. Define Problem
When I find an issue, it's usually just one instance of a deeper, bigger problem. For this reason, I like to keep asking myself 'why' to identify the underlying problem.
